About me

Hi! I’m Bryce Verberne, a Computer Science major currently pursuing a Bachelor’s degree with a concentration in Software Engineering at Arizona State University. I am an enthusiast for both technology and aerospace, and am excited by the idea to one day have a career that combines the two. An area that has particularly captured my fascination is telecommunications, which is shown in the various projects that I’ve undertaken. I am currently a Software Engineer at the Sun Devil Satellite Laboratory at ASU, where I work on the Coconut CubeSat partnered with NASA, a 1U satellite that will be launched into orbit between 2024 and 2027.

Outside of ASU, I enjoy filling my freetime with various other projects, which includes the SatComm Optimizer and my experience with the NASA's L’SPACE Mission Concept Academy (MCA). SatComm Optimizer is a personal project aiming to improve satellite communication networks with computer simulations and advanced algorithms. And during my time at NASA, I worked as a Deputy Project Manager and Computer Hardware Engineer, where I spearheaded a 12-member team of passionate university students across the US and designed the CDH subsystem for a Mars rover mission concept.

Amongst these various experiences, I’ve obtained an in-depth knowledge of leadership, teaming, programming, embedded systems, and various other skills and experiences that are discussed further in my skills and projects outlined below.

Coconut CubeSat

April 2024 - Present

As a Software Engineer for the Sun Devil Satellite Laboratory at ASU, I work on the Coconut CubeSat partnered with NASA. This is a 1U satellite built to relay information from remote sensing devices back to the ASU ground station. This project was selected by NASA's CubeSat Launch Initiative (CSLI) and will be launched into orbit between 2024 and 2027.

Learn More

SatComm Optimizer

May 2024 - Present

SatComm Optimizer is a personal project, designed to enhance satellite communication networks by developing advanced simulation models. Taking into account factors such as bandwidth and signal latency, it utilizes sophisticated algorithms and data visualization to analyze and optimize network performance.

Learn More

NASA L'SPACE MCA

Jan 2024 - April 2024

At the NASA L'SPACE Mission Concept Academy (MCA), I led a 12-member team as Deputy Project Manager and designed the command and data handling subsystem as the computer hardware engineer for the Mars rover mission concept, Endurance. This mission concept was one of the highest scoring for the term - a rare and notable achievement.
